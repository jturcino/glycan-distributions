Welcome!

Here, you can quantify and visualize the differences in site-specific glycosylation between glycoproteins. Click the "submit form" link in the sidebar to get started! If you'd like some more information on what we're doing, keep reading.

Setting up

We quantify differences in site-specific glycosylation using probability distribution distance metrics. The program requires three inputs for two glycoproteins

  • GlycReSoft glycopeptide CSV files
  • UniProt accession ID
  • Dataset name

We label one dataset as "expected" and the other as "experimental"; however, the reason for the labeling is defined by the user. Some examples include

  • A protein (expected) and its mutant (experimental)
  • A control sample (expected) and a treatment sample (experimental)

Comparing glycan populations

The glycopeptides for each dataset are quality filtered and a glycan population is defined for each proteinâ€™s glycosylation sites. Those populations are then normalized to produce probability distributions representing the likelihood of a glycan appearing at a particular glycosylation site. We match sequons to align glycosylation sites between proteins and compare glycan probability distributions using cosine similarity (CS) and normalized Kullback-Leibler divergence (nKLD).

Cosine similarity

CS is simply the cosine of the angle between two n-dimensional vectors. It ranges from zero to one, with a higher value meaning the vectors are more similar. For glycan probability distributions, a CS of zero means the paired distributions have no glycans in common; a CS of one means the distributions are identical.

Normalized Kullback-Leibler divergence

nKLD quantifies how well the expected distribution predicts the behavior of the experimental distribution. It also ranges from zero to one, but unlike CS a low value means the distributions are similar. For glycan probability distributions, a nKLD of zero means the distributions are identical; a nKLD of one means the expected distribution does not relate to the experimental distribution.
